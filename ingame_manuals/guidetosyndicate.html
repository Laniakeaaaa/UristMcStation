Being an Antagonist

    So you think you have what it takes to be an antagonist? The person everyone hates, everyone is gunning for, often with no friends, allies or people to trust? Being an antagonist is hard,

you will get perforated by a submachine gun, turned into ash by laser guns, stunned then beaten by security, or have every joe schmoe assistant try to be a hero and take a shot at you.

    The road of being an antagonist is one full of failures and mishaps, you will get discouraged, laughed at for being unrobust, but fear not! Being an antagonist is also incredibly rewarding, when your trap

is sprung with perfection, all things go to plan, when you live through moments that are right out of an action movie and everyone remembers you as the one who let them have a great round are

some of the most memorable and rewarding experiences in SS13. But, it is a very daunting responsibility to be in charge of making a round fun, believe me most people who know me, know very well I have the same fuck ups and disasters that most antagonists face. I will not claim to be a great antagonist, nor a robust one but I hope this guide will help new and veteran players alike make informed decisions on being an antagonist.

Part

2

Rolling Traitor

    Whether by blackmail, a bad upbringing, an unpaid debt, threat of death, or all of the above you are now in the service of one of the nebulous corporations under The Syndicate. Congratulations, along with this you are given a PDA full of gadgets, guns, explosives fuck you bay, grenades and useless items to show that you can get the job done without them. As a traitor, you arguably have one of the most classic roles in SS13 and will usually be your most common antag role.

    You will not be alone however, other agents are usually dispatched to the station as well. You can attempt to identify them with the oh so subtle codewords, or if you see some lunatic shooting up the security office you can probably guess they are a fellow operative. Working with fellow operatives is a rare but rewarding experience and usually gives you much more leeway in your actions, however usually you should expect to be alone. Being a traitor is also the most freeform, the sky's the limit for what you want to do to make the job interesting. Start a hostage situation by kidnapping the captain? Be a mad bomber and send everyone to a cold grave in the void of space? Leave a trail of murders for security to solve? It's all up to you really, and along with that the tools in your PDA are incredibly useful and give you the utility to accomplish any task you want. However, there are no refunds. Knowing which tool does what and it's true utility aside from the terrible 2 sentence blurb you get on your PDA comes with experience, but hopefully this guide can help you make an informed decision on what items to buy.

Choosing the Right Equipment

AMMUNITION:

Ammunition, bullets for your guns. They are usually fairly reasonably priced, with stronger calibres being more expensive than weaker rounds. Generally, most of the ammunition here besides the rarer niche

calibres such as flechettes can be created in a hacked autolathe and printed for free, so only buy ammunition when necessary for example if you can't access an autolathe or intend to have a spare reload on the spot.

BADASSERY:

Should be noted this will consume all of your telecrystals, but you can get a single balloon in syndicate red or nanotrasen blue. Truly, a worthwhile purchase (no really don't do this unless you are absolutely confident in your abilities to have no need for equipment from your PDA).

Once upon a time, for a brief day, the money cannon when introduced was the godslayer. Heads exploded into fine red mist when hit by a gigantic ball of 1000000 thalers fired from this stylish, sleek red hand cannon. Formerly the most dangerous weapon on SS13, it's brief position as the most robust weapon has long been gone and now you will just tickle most people when you fire wads of cash at their face.

However, it is very funny and if you have a gimmick as some philanthropic millionaire pelting people in the hallway with wads of fast stacks, then by all means buy it. Just don't expect to kill anyone with it.

DEVICES AND TOOLS:

  • Fully Loaded Toolbox
  • TC Cost: 8
  • A toolbox in stylish red-black syndicate colours with a full set of tools, but most importantly includes a pair of insulated gloves. Tools can be acquired completely legally, but if you are in a job that doesn't have easy access to insulated gloves this is a very worthwhile buy. As an added bonus, the toolbox also deals more damage compared to the regular one, for that authentic greytide experience.
  • Operations Funding
  • TC Cost: 8
  • A briefcase with 10000 thalers. Generally quite hard to amass large amounts of money ingame, and you will usually use this for bribery purposes to enlist the more criminally inclined crewmembers to help your plan. This can include bribing them to frame a coworker, steal items for you, or even to pretend to be your hostage in some elaborate insurance scheme.
  • Morphic Clerical Kit
  • TC Cost: 16
  • Contains a stamp, tagger, pen and labeller. Why you would ever need to use this is beyond me, but it can forge a captain's stamp.
  • C-4
  • TC Cost: 16
  • The classic brick of C4. Slap it on a wall and make a stylish exit or entrance. While there are easier ways to break into places, there are none as flashy. You cannot actually slap it on people now and even detonated in a backpack it does a pitiful explosion. Also now one of the rare few explosives in your arsenal, generally if you need a 1 tile entrance it is a safe buy.
  • Heavy Armor Vest and Helmet
  • TC Cost: 16
  • A heavy full body suit that gives fairly equal protection from ballistics and lasers, looks good and for a cheap price. While similar protection can be attained in the armory, armory break ins are usually fairly risky and you will most likely have to contend with security. Without armor if you didn't buy this. Even if you never intend on going violent and loud, it never hurts to have a set of armor stashed somewhere for a rainy day. In SS13 good protection is king.

  • Encrypted Radio Channel Key
  • TC Cost: 1
  • A key for private communication for you and other operatives. Doesn't see a lot of use, because it can be substituted by changing your radio channel to one of the dozens of unused ones, but for a single TC and convenient communication it is worth it if you have someone to communicate with.
  • Shield Diffuser
  • TC Cost: 16
  • Allows you to bypass the outer ship shields if they are set to block organics from coming in. Unless you expect to get locked out of the ship, or engineers build a crazy fortress, there will likely be very little use for this.
  • Suit Sensor Jamming Device
  • TC Cost: 20
  • Blocks the suit sensors of people in a certain radius. If you intend on doing crimes, your main issue will be bystanders screaming into the radio than suit sensors. Also besides a select few individuals like my friend and coauthor Anders, not many people obsessively monitor them so the worth of this item is debatable.
  • Binary Translator Key
  • TC Cost: 20
  • Allows you to tap into the radio channel that silicons such as the AI and cyborgs use. Can be fairly useful if there are silicons playing that round, and often they will be a fairly high priority threat due to the AI being one of the few roles that will be constantly monitoring the station.
  • Cryptographic Sequencer
  • TC: 24
  • Nicknamed the "emag", has a limited number of uses but usually has very interesting effects on most electronics. On consoles or vendors unlocks their overrides and reveals contraband items, on most helper bots such as Beepsky/Securitons or floorbots will turn them randomly aggressive to everyone, quickly breaks open doors and a multitude of other easter eggs in game. A good buy with plenty of utility, just be wary that it has a limited number of uses before breaking.
  • Thermal Imaging Glasses
  • TC: 24
  • Disguised as the optical mesons miners use and not appearing to be contraband to security, it is very useful to see crewmembers and cyborgs/mobs through walls allowing you to always stay one step ahead of probing security or crewmembers. An extremely worthwhile buy, letting you to evade or prepare to fight security, alert you to incoming crew and generally keeps you one step ahead of everyone. Should be noted that this leaves you very vulnerable to flashes and their effect will now damage your eyes.

  • Radio Interceptor
  • Broken fucking piece of bay shit never buy this completely worthless, it's just a regular radio.
  • Space Suit
  • TC Cost: 28
  • Syndicate red-black voidsuit that is the exact same that nuclear operatives use. Provides decent protection, void protection, and all the benefits a hardsuit has of no slow down and good suit storage. Quite good for 28 TC, slightly less protective and more expensive than the heavy vest but it allows you to go into space.
  • Door Hacking Tool
  • TC Cost: 24
  • Incredibly unintuitive but lots of utility. Screwdriver it once (no message will be shown) to put it into hack mode. Click on it to an airlock to begin hacking, after around 10-20 seconds it will succeed and you gain access to full door panel functions like electrify, bolts, open, lights etc. The last 5-8 airlocks you hack will be instantly accessible with the tool. Very rarely used but a good buy.
  • Flashdark
  • TC Cost: 32
  • Previously a very unknown item due to it's confusing description, it basically engulfs the user in a circle of darkness that makes them harder to see and target by enemies. Used to be extremely effective even in contact with direct light, now whenever it encounters a light source it has little to no effectiveness. Prior to nerf very powerful, now likely too niche besides for gimmick purposes to be a black ball of darkness.
  • Powersink
  • TC Cost: 40
  • Drains the station of power, anchor it down on a live wire to use. Same effect can be done by sabotaging the engine, but this is more discreet and does it's job of draining the station of power. Can be useful if the intent is to depower the station for whatever reason.
  • Hacked Supply Beacon
  • TC Cost: 52
  • After anchored down onto a live wire, when activated will spawn a very large drop pod from the sky to its location. Inside it contains a random assortment of cargo items, ranging from animals to guns. If you like to roll the dice and have a very cool drop pod spawned then it can be a useful by, but there are likely more practical uses for it's high TC cost.
  • Camera MIU
  • TC Cost: 60
  • When activated you are stationary but have access to every single camera network with seamless transition, similar to an AI. Likely worth it's hefty cost due to the incredible utility and information potential, buy if surveillance of the station is needed.

Grenades:

  • 1x Smoke Grenade
  • TC Cost: 4
  • A smoke grenade for stylish exits and entrances. Cheap and can help you escape a gang of chasing security.
  • 1x Photon Disruption Grenade
  • TC Cost: 4
  • Likely more use than the smoke grenade, in a very wide radius disables all light sources and forces pitch darkness, lasts longer than the smoke grenade.
  • 1x EMP Grenade
  • TC Cost: 8
  • Apply to silicons and technology for best results. Great for trying to kill an AI or an angry IPC, useful if no ion weapons are available.
  • 5x Smoke Grenades
  • TC Cost: 16
  • Bundled discount deal for buying in bulk. Not bad if planning to buy multiple grenades.
  • 5x Photon Disruption Grenades
  • TC Cost: 16
  • Bundled discount deal for buying in bulk. Not bad if planning to buy multiple grenades.
  • 5x EMP Grenades
  • TC Cost: 24
  • Bundled discount deal for buying in bulk. Not bad if planning to buy multiple grenades.

HARDSUIT MODULES:

  • Thermal Scanner
  • TC Cost: 16
  • Cheaper than regular thermal scanners, a good buy.
  • Net projector
  • TC Cost: 20
  • Niche use, nets targets where they cannot move or escape the net for a duration, good for pacifistic gimmicks and relatively cheap cost.
  • Electrowarfare Suite and Voice Synthesizer
  • TC Cost: 24
  • Module that is both an inbuilt voice changer and several modules to fight against the enemy AI. Already cheaper than a voice changer with utility modules to fight the AI, a worthwhile buy.
  • Maneuvering Jets
  • TC Cost: 32
  • Expensive, let's you move in space without a jetpack. Can be useful but likely better to save the TCs and opt for a jetpack or throwing items instead, many alternatives.
  • Power Sink
  • TC Cost: 48
  • Mobile power sink, more expensive than the stationary but easier to use, if have access to a hardsuit could be a worthwhile buy.
  • Mounted Energy Gun
  • TC Cost: 48
  • Mounted infinite ammo (relatively, until suit power runs out) energy gun that is also cheaper than the advanced energy gun. Like most hardsuit items, offers same utility as other items for a cheaper cost.
  • Mounted Laser Cannon
  • TC Cost: 64
  • Same as the mounted energy gun but instead fires more damaging laser cannon bolts for higher cost. Not a bad deal, up to the user to buy.

IMPLANTS:

  • Freedom Implant
  • TC Cost: 24
  • After implanting, you can choose the emote that will trigger you immediately breaking out of your cuffs. Has a limited usage of 3-5 times. Incredibly useful and usually not expected, this can allow you to have a second wind and escape arrest after losing a fight with security, or allow you to fill your arresting officer with lead before they realize what's happening. Highly recommended.
  • Compressed Matter Implant
  • TC Cost: 32
  • Allows you to store any item that can fit in a box (small items) into your face, which can't really be searched by security. Can be helpful, to smuggle a door hacking tool to escape prison as a backup plan. Situational use, but it can be helpful for the cautious traitor who always has a backup plan to get back into the game.
  • Explosive Implant
  • TC Cost: 40
  • For when you want to go out with a bang, or you want to turn some poor sap into a living bomb.  Has 3 options, localized, body explosion or full explosion. Localized will just gib the limb or appendage it was implanted in, body explosion will just kill the implantee with no damage to surroundings, while full explosion will destroy the body and damage the surroundings, but not enough to cause a hull breach. It is activated by a chosen codeword, when either you or anyone in the surrounding area or radio speaks the codeword it will detonate the implant. Suffice to be said, don't make it your own name unless you intend on blowing up. Recommended, but use with caution.
  • Uplink Implant
  • TC Cost: 50% of current remaining TC
  • Allows you to access your uplink at any time instead of being reliant on your PDA, but at the cost of 50% less TC. Potential when captured by security and put into the brig, but the very high cost could likely be better spent buying items to prevent being captured in the first place. Not recommended.
  • Imprinting Implant
  • TC Cost: 20
  • Basically slaves any unfortunate victim to a predetermined lawset defined by you. Incredible potential, with pretty much limitless opportunities for creativity. Make the person serve your bidding, or slave multiple people and set them all to murder each other and watch the carnage. Added bonus of involving people in the round which, is always a good thing and often can be great fun for other people. Should be noted that like making laws for an AI, you should be careful with your wording because people will get around it. Set your laws on the imprinter, inject victim with LSD, take the chip from the imprinter with the implanter. Highly recommended.

STEALTH AND CAMO ITEMS:

  • No Slip Shoes
  • TC Cost: 4
  • Shoes that don't slip on wet surfaces, soap or the clown's banana. Similar to galoshes but the shoes don't have any slowdown, making them much more effective than actual galoshes. May seem insignificant until you get slipped by the janitor during a chase with security then beaten to death by his mop. Don't let this happen to you, buy no slip shoes. Almost a must buy personally every round.
  • Agent ID Card
  • TC Cost: 12
  • Basically a custom ID card. You can set any job title you desire, the name of the person holding the ID, how the ID looks like etc. Comes with the functionality that lets you copy any access of another ID card by sliding yours on top of it. Used in many gimmicks to maintain anonymity, combine with the voice changer to remain fully anonymous and taunt the crew over radio with any edgy moniker you can come up with. Recommended.
  • Chameleon Kit
  • TC Cost: 20
  • Contains a hat, mask, exosuit, jumpsuit, gloves, shoes and a gun that you can customize the appearance of to match every single item in the game, no kidding. For those fashion minded antagonists or those who want to dress up as Santa Claus as you mow down security with an assault rifle. Should be noted that when transformed none of these items actually provide protection, they merely take on the appearance of anything you desire. The gun is also fake, but it can be funny scaring security with a machine gun that fires BB pellets. Recommended, another item that can be fairly helpful in gimmicks.
  • Voice Changer
  • TC Cost: 20
  • Companion item to the Agent ID Card, this gasmask allows you to take on any voice you please, you choose the name. Impersonate the captain, your kidnapped target, use it to frame someone, the possibilities are endless. Recommended.
  • Chameleon Projector
  • TC Cost: 32
  • This can be used to scan any item, then when activating it in your hand you will assume that appearance. Perfect for evading security, you disguise yourself as anything from banana peels to cigarette butts and get practically walked over by security with none the wiser. Just make sure no one tries to pick you up, because then the disguise field is broken and you'll be caught with your pants down. Recommended.

  • Human Suit
  • TC Cost: 5
  • A rubber suit to hide your race and make you look like a Keshi Skin Bandit. At a glance you'll fool some people into thinking you're human but most people who examine you will probably see you wearing a rubber mask and suit attempting to relate to your "fellow humans". It's incredibly cheap and can also make for a funny/incredibly creepy disguise though, so not a bad choice to buy for fun or a gimmick.

  • Sneakies
  • TC Cost: 4
  • Shoes that when worn make your footprints through blood, oil, spilled fruit etc. appear to be you walking backwards. If you ever find a detective who can deduce who the murderer is by the direction of their footprints then you might as well turn yourself in because they have the deduction skills of Sherlock Holmes. Pretty much a joke item, buy it if you wish just don’t expect any practical benefit.
  • Smuggler’s Satchel
  • TC Cost: 8
  • A small satchel with little storage space that can be placed and hidden beneath floor tiles. Likely useful for stashing away items or to plant bombs anywhere, also potential to easily hide tools in the permabrig for easy escapes. Not too expensive and potential utility, not a bad buy.
  • Bug Kit
  • TC Cost: 16
  • As of now bugged and without a sprite, don’t buy it. In theory would allow you to place bugs around the station to remotely monitor locations.

MEDICAL

  • Box of Sin Pockets
  • TC Cost: 8
  • It’s a box with syndicate brand hot pockets that you can heat up without a microwave by crushing the donk pocket. Truly worth every single telecrystal, the perfect snack for every traitor. Buy this if you are incredibly hungry I guess, each pocket contains nutriment and animal protein.
  • Surgery Kit
  • TC Cost: 40
  • Generally, if you’re injured enough to need surgery self-surgery is not the greatest idea and is usually incredibly finicky. Spawns an entire kit with all the tools you need to do surgery, including an advanced trauma kit. Could be useful if you’re doing a gimmick where you kidnap people and do back alley surgeries to harvest their organs in maintenance, or in the very rare occasion your friend needs surgery and you can’t go to medbay. Very niche uses though, for a hefty cost.
  • Combat Medical Kit
  • TC Cost: 48
  • A box that contains pill bottles of: Bicardine, Dermaline, Dexalin Plus, Dylovene, Tramadol, Spaceallin and medical splints. Not exactly pretty rare stuff that you couldn’t have gotten just ransacking medbay or making it yourself with a chemistry. Fairly expensive and likely not worth the cost to attain items easily gotten on station.

HIGHLY VISIBLE AND DANGEROUS WEAPONS:

Small Calibre Weapons:

  • Energy Crossbow
  • TC Cost: 24
  • Magazine Capacity: 8
  • Self-Recharging? Yes
  • Damage: 8.5 toxin
  • Normal sized, easy to use and hits easily without baymiss. Also silenced, while it will not kill or stun someone immediately with enough shots it will cause eventual organ failure in the victim, also noticeable is the lack of recoil. Good buy for cheap cost.
  • Energy Gun
  • TC Cost: 32
  • Magazine Capacity: 10
  • Self-Recharging? No
  • Damage: ~8 burn + Pain For Shock/4 shots KD Stun/40 Burn Kill
  • No recoil like all energy weapons, hitscan weapon. Fairly high damage with wide range of utility, however finding a way to recharge it may be difficult. Add in the extra 18 TC to buy the advanced energy gun if desiring an energy weapon with multiple options for detaining or killing.
  • Exosuit (APLU) Rigged Laser
  • TC Cost: 32
  • Magazine Capacity: Infinite (Based on suit power)
  • Self-Recharging? Yes
  • Damage: ~30 burn
  • Very slow to fire and requires a RIPLEY, which is both difficult to attain and extremely slow. Likely won't be killing anyone with this and you're better off buying a regular gun, not recommended.
  • Silenced Holdout Pistol
  • TC Cost: 32
  • Magazine Capacity: 8
  • Damage: 25 brute
  • Calibre: 9mm
  • Small sized, fits in your pocket without the silencer, being a pistol can be fired with one hand with no accuracy penalty. A cheap ballistic gun with cheap ammo (3 TC) each with a stealth option, with the added bonus of looking cool. Recommended.

  • Energy Sword
  • TC Cost: 32
  • Damage: 30 brute
  • Small sized when unactivated, can fit in your pocket, and unlike most cutting weapons does not embed into your opponent however it has a small chance of breaking like most. Personally guns are usually more useful in combat, energy swords look cool but some people prefer melee, so it is a worthwhile weapon. Caution is that while 2 swords can be combined to a double bladed, it is bugged and becomes steel material, allowing it to easily break and embed in opponents while still doing the same damage.
  • Holdout Revolver
  • TC Cost: 38
  • Magazine Capacity: 6
  • Damage: 25 brute
  • Calibre: .38
  • Normal sized, 6 round capacity being a revolver with medium pistol damage. Fairly expensive with no added benefit over the silenced holdout pistol, better off spending more TC on a stronger gun or buying the cheaper silenced holdout pistol. Not recommended.

Medium Calibre/Cost Weapons

  • Sawnoff Shotgun
  • TC Cost: 45
  • Magazine Capacity: 2
  • Damage: 5-10*7 brute with 1 less pellet per tile of distance for buckshot/60 brute slugs
  • While this can be obtained on station by stealing the bartender's shotgun and sawing it down, this may not always be an option. Very respectable gun, it can compare to the other full size pump shotguns by being able to fire 2 rounds consecutively without pumping, or 2 barrels at the same time. Devastating with slugs, the high damage allows it to pierce armour easier and cause severe organ and bone damage. Will gib limbs in 2-3 hits, a good buy.
  • 9mm Pistol
  • TC Cost: 40
  • Magazine Capacity: 15
  • Damage: 25 brute
  • Calibre: 9mm
  • Normal size, same as the silenced holdout pistol but with double the magazine capacity. Personal preference, while more TCs will give a much stronger gun the extra magazine capacity and cheaper cost allows for more forgiveness when aiming.

  • Machine Pistol
  • TC Cost: 45
  • Magazine Capacity: 16
  • Damage: 25 brute
  • Calibre: .45
  • The classic uzi style pistol, it has semi-auto, 3-round burst and 5 round burst capabilities that gives it better crowd control compared to the similar 9mm pistol. Not a bad gun, but the low magazine capacity and damage will make it heavy difficulty fighting armoured opponents, and for a few TC more stronger guns exist. Buy on preference.
  • .44 Revolver
  • TC Cost: 48
  • Magazine Capacity: 6
  • Damage: 30 brute
  • Calibre: .44
  • Another revolver, however little improvement on damage compared to other guns in it's range, low magazine capacity and fairly high expensive makes this not recommended, cheaper guns offer the same or better and more expensive guns offer much bigger improvements in damage.

Heavy Weapons

  • .50 Magnum
  • TC Cost: 52
  • Magazine Capacity: 7
  • Damage: ~60 brute
  • Calibre: .50 AE
  • An absolute beast of a gun, high recoil but if you can control it the bullets hit like a truck. Reaches the damage threshold where 2-3 shots will maim or gib limbs, an extremely lethal gun that will cause massive organ and bone damage at anything you point it at. Highly recommended.
  • Combat Shotgun
  • TC Cost: 52
  • Magazine Capacity: 7 in the tube with 1 in chamber = 8
  • Damage: 5-10*7 brute with 1 less pellet per tile of distance for buckshot/60 brute slugs
  • Calibre: Buckshot/Slugs
  • With 8 slugs the rack of your shotgun will be the fear of most security teams. Comparable to other guns of this tier, again personal preference. While the rate of fire is lower to the side arms due to the need to pump the shotgun for every shot, slugs hit harder with more armour piercing and is also an aesthetic preference. Bulky weapon, the lack of concealment usually means once this gun is out the only viable next step is shooting up security. Highly recommended.
  • Submachine Gun
  • TC Cost: 52
  • Magazine Capacity: 20
  • Damage: 28 brute
  • Calibre: 10mm
  • Formerly known as the C20-r, this is the famed iconic weapon of nuclear operatives everywhere. With semi auto, 3 round burst and 5 round burst capabilities it has great crowd control capabilities and with it's large magazine size is capable of providing suppressive fire. Being a large sized weapon it is still portable in backpacks so it can be concealed. Recommended for those who prefer faster firing weapons and better crowd control capabilities.
  • .357 Revolver
  • TC Cost: 56
  • Magazine Capacity: 6
  • Damage: ~60 brute
  • Calibre: .357
  • Almost identical to the .50 Magnum besides aesthetic difference and differences in reloading. Entirely a matter of preference of which one to choose, generally can't go wrong choosing either of these. Highly recommended.
  • Flechette Rifle
  • TC Cost: 60
  • Magazine Capacity: 10
  • Damage: 20-30 brute
  • Calibre: Flechettes
  • Lacks recoil
  • A very unique and futuristic looking rifle, it is the only rifle in the game to fire flechettes. While appearing to be low damage, the unique property of flechettes is that it will deal nearly full damage against all kinds of armour, even the famed full body ballistic suits. The absolutely absurd penetration values on the flechettes mean there is no real counter to the gun besides waiting for it to run empty with the low magazine size. Should be noted these flechettes unlike most ballistics are hard to obtain outside of the uplink, due to the autolathe not stocking them. Has semi auto and 5 round burst capabilities, it's a niche choice but deadly in the right hands.

  • Advanced Energy Gun
  • TC Cost: 60
  • Magazine Capacity: 10
  • Self-Recharging? Yes
  • Damage: ~8 burn + Pain For Shock/4 shots KD Stun/40 Burn Kill
  • Identical to the regular energy gun besides that it is self-recharging, giving it a huge advantage over most conventional weapons. Favours a hit and run style of gameplay, and practically a necessity if planning on using stun weapons seriously due to the impracticality of carrying several tasers. Highly recommended.
  • Pulse Rifle
  • TC Cost: 68
  • Magazine Capacity: 36 but only 3 round burst capability, lacking semi-auto
  • Self-Recharging? No
  • Damage: 50 burn damage per shot, 150 potential total with 3 round burst
  • One of the rare few burst fire energy weapons, at 150 potential burn damage for each burst this massive damage potential will make short work of most opponents. Reasonably priced for it's damage and the most superior option for energy weapons if intending to kill. Recommended.
  • Assault Rifle
  • TC Cost: 60
  • Magazine Capacity: 20
  • Damage: 30
  • Calibre: 5.56 mm
  • The closest thing in game to a space AK-47, formerly used 7.62 mm but was switched because Bay likes doing weird shit like that. Rifle calibre bullets give it the unique property of having higher penetration against armour in comparison to pistol rounds, and it can penetrate and hit multiple targets through flesh. Comes in semi auto, 3 round burst and 5 round burst capabilities, most security officers who see you will be scrambling for cover. Recommended.

COMPLETELY OVERKILL WEAPONS

These weapons are incredibly expensive and will take up most, if not all of your TCs, with most of them being fairly impractical for extended use due to all the ammunition requiring to be bought with the uplink. When you buy these weapons you resign yourself to have a round solely dedicated to slaughter the station, which is frowned upon. And I really mean slaughter, all of these weapons will likely kill anyone on the first hit through any armour. Use these weapons responsibly, going past this point lies the road of salt, tears and regret.

  • Anti-material Rifle AKA PTSD Gun
  • TC Cost: 96
  • Magazine Capacity: 1
  • Damage: 80 Damage APDS/Does It Really Matter The Dudes Head is Gone Regular
  • Calibre: 14.5 mm /14.5 APDS
  • The terror of the station, the only sniper rifle available in the uplink. Extreme long range scope that can be combined with thermal goggles to make someone's head disappear across the station through a reinforced wall. Absurd penetration let's it penetrate reinforced walls with ease, and one shot is capable of going through as many as 3 bodies to maim an entire room. Most shots will outright kill the target in a single shot. Every time you use this accept that everyone will be incredibly salty post round and you bring it another step to the nerfhammer.
  • Rail Gun
  • TC Cost: 130
  • Magazine Capacity: 10
  • Damage: 75 brute
  • Calibre: Matter cartridges
  • Semi auto capability only, and will literally consume all of your TCs so I hope you are satisfied with your choice. Can be reloaded with very expensive matter cartridges, however only has 2-3 reloads worth of power in the cell before it needs to be recharged. But to be honest, by that point everyone and your dignity is dead anyways so it doesn't matter.
  • Advanced Rail Gun
  • TC Cost: 151
  • Magazine Capacity: 6
  • Damage: 55 brute
  • Calibre: Metal rods
  • You will likely never use this gun, because aside from a discount this gun costs more than all of your TCs combined. The only way possible to attain this gun is if you find someone who hates the station just as much as you do and is willing to share their TCs. With incredibly easy to attain ammunition, high penetration through multiple targets suffice to be said you use this gun whenever you truly hate everyone on board the station.

Final Tips and Ending Thoughts

Tips:

  • Until you’re ready to get chased by security, keep items stashed somewhere safe so if you do get searched you don’t lose everything
  • There are randomized discounts on random items in your uplink that can let you do very funny things, like buy 15 fragmentation bombs for 2 TC each and play a round of bomberman (yes this did actually happen back when I played on Baystation). Keep an eye out for them, and if you aren’t in a rush to advance the round, waiting around 15-30 minutes after round start couldn’t hurt. Everyone could get lucky.
  • A good rule of thumb is, the more equipment you have on you, the more close quarters spaces disfavours you. While your opponent has really nothing to lose when they try to disarm and push you, you have the advantage with your weapons at range. Be cautious when engaging people in close quarters.
  • Your job first and foremost is generally for everyone to have fun. There are a few good general guidelines that I personally try to follow that I find are just good to keep things less salty for both parties.
  • Unless a civilian is actively trying to fuck you over, or they’re part of your gimmick/objective, give them a chance to scram if they see you or use aim mode to take them hostage. Security is usually fair game for all on sight shooting since they do the same for you.
  • Combat is random and unpredictable, but when the dust settles, if you’re still standing and the other guy is breathing but KO’ed, try not to execute him. Exceptions obviously apply, but it’s just something good to keep in mind with the mindset of keeping people in the round
  • Along with that, you’re much like the Joker, he only has purpose when he has someone like Batman to try to stop him. In a sense, rounds are actually easier with security on board for you because you have someone to continue the conflict and pull less punches for. It’s less satisfying to bully a securityless crew who can’t fight back.
  • I’m usually a fairly cautious player, but always have backup plans. Everything in SS13 goes to shit and nothing goes to plan. Running out of ammunition while 4 security officers are enroute to your location is probably an experience that will teach you that you never have enough ammunition
